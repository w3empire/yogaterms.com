What does 'Om' mean?

author Anshu Sengupta

Om, also spelled Aum, is a sacred sound and a spiritual symbol in Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism. It is considered to be the sound of the universe and is often used in meditation as a means of reaching a higher state of consciousness.

In yoga, Om is often chanted at the beginning and end of a practice as a way to connect with the divine and bring a sense of peace and unity to the mind and body. Along with that, Om is believed to have a powerful vibration that can help to balance and align the chakras, or energy centers, in the body.

More information

Om is a sacred syllable that is believed to represent the ultimate reality or consciousness. It is considered to be the sound of the universe and is thought to be the fundamental vibration that underlies all of existence.

In Hinduism, Om is seen as a representation of the divine and is often used in rituals and ceremonies to invoke the divine. In Buddhism, Om is used as a tool for meditation and is believed to help practitioners achieve a deeper state of awareness and understanding.

In yoga, Om is often used as a mantra, which is a word or sound that is repeated during meditation. Chanting Om is thought to help quiet the mind and bring a sense of peace and unity to the body and mind. Additionally, Om is believed to have a powerful vibration that can help to balance and align the chakras, or energy centers, in the body.

Om is also a symbol with three phonetic parts: A., U., and M., which are believed to represent the three states of consciousness:

  • “A” represents the waking state,
  • “U” represents the dream state,
  • “M” represents the deep sleep state.

Some people also believe that “Om” represents the three aspects of time: past, present, and future.

What are the meanings of the three phonetic parts of Om?

The three phonetic parts of Om are said to represent the three states of consciousness in Hinduism and other Eastern traditions, with the “A” representing the waking state, the “U” representing the dream state, and the “M” representing the deep sleep state.

The sound of “A” is said to represent the state of wakefulness, where the mind is active and engaged with the external world. The “U” is said to represent the dream state, where the mind is active but not engaged with the external world, and the “M” is said to represent the deep sleep state, where the mind is at rest and not active.

These states of consciousness are understood to be different aspects of the ultimate reality and consciousness, which is considered to be divine.

Apart from that, it is also believed that they represent the three aspects of divine consciousness: creation, preservation, and destruction. The “A” is considered to represent creation, the “U” preservation, and the “M” destruction. These three aspects of divine consciousness represent the cycle of birth, existence, and dissolution.

People think that by chanting Om, they can connect with this divine consciousness and learn more about how reality really works.
