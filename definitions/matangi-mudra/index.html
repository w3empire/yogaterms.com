What does Matangi Mudra mean?

author Juhi Thakkar

Matangi mudra is a hand gesture used in the practice of yoga and meditation. The word “mudra” means “gesture” or “seal” in Sanskrit, and it refers to specific hand gestures that are believed to have various spiritual and psychological benefits.

In the case of Matangi mudra, the gesture is made by pressing the tips of the thumb, index, and middle fingers together, while keeping the other two fingers extended. This mudra is associated with the throat chakra, which is associated with communication and self-expression. It is said to help with verbal expression and communication, and to help cultivate inner peace and calmness.

More information

There are a few steps you can follow to practice Matangi mudra:

  • Find a comfortable seated position, either on a chair or on the floor with your legs crossed.
  • Take a few deep breaths to relax and bring your awareness to your body and breath.
  • Place your hands on your knees or thighs, with your palms facing upward.
  • Bend your index, middle, and thumb fingers in toward your palm, while keeping your other two fingers extended.
  • Hold this mudra for a few minutes, focusing on your breath and the sensation of the mudra in your hands.
  • As you hold the mudra, visualize a blue light emanating from your throat chakra, which is located at the base of your neck. This light is said to help open and activate the throat chakra, promoting clear communication and self-expression.
  • When you are ready, release the mudra and take a few deep breaths to ground yourself.

It’s important to keep in mind that mudras are not a substitute for medical treatment, and you should always consult with a healthcare professional if you have any concerns about your health.

Additionally, it’s best to practice mudras under the guidance of a trained teacher who can help you learn the correct technique and incorporate them into your yoga or meditation practice.
