What does 'Rasa' mean?

author Talia Luettgen

Rasa is a term from Indian aesthetics that refers to the emotional flavor or essence of a work of art, literature, or performance. In classical Indian literature, rasa is considered one of the most important aspects of a work of art, and the goal of many works is to evoke particular emotional responses in the audience.

In Indian philosophy and religion, rasa is also considered to be a spiritual experience or state of consciousness. In Ayurveda, rasa is one of the seven bodily tissues that support the body.

In yoga, “rasa” can refer to the emotional or spiritual experiences that are said to be achieved through the practice of yoga. The ultimate goal of yoga is to achieve a state of spiritual transcendence or enlightenment, and it is believed that different yoga practices and techniques can evoke different emotional or spiritual experiences, or “rasas.”

Spiritual traditions

In spiritual traditions such as Indian philosophy and religion, rasa is often seen as the essence of human experience. It is believed that the emotions and feelings that govern human life are key to understanding the nature of reality and achieving spiritual transcendence.

The ultimate goal of spiritual practices is to experience transcendent emotions such as love, devotion, peace, and joy. In Indian aesthetics, rasa is considered one of the most important aspects of a work of art, and the goal of many works is to evoke particular emotional responses in the audience.

In Indian mythology, Rasa is also associated with the concept of “Rasa Lila,” which refers to the divine play of Lord Krishna and Radha, in which they experience and express different emotions. These emotions are seen as representations of the different aspects of the human experience, and the goal is to transcend them and achieve spiritual liberation.

Rasa experiences are thought to lead to spiritual enlightenment and liberation in some Indian spiritual traditions. The ultimate goal is to experience the highest rasa, known as “Ananda” or “bliss,” which represents the ultimate unity with the divine.

Human emotions

The nine basic human emotions, or Rasas, in Indian aesthetics are:

  1. Sringara (love, unity, and beauty): This Rasa evokes feelings of love and unity, as well as appreciation for beauty. It is associated with romantic love and beauty in all forms.
  2. Hasya (humor, joy): This Rasa evokes feelings of joy and humor. It is associated with laughter and the ability to find humor in everyday life.
  3. Adbhuta (inquisitiveness, wonder): This Rasa evokes feelings of wonder and inquisitiveness. It is associated with the sense of wonder and amazement that one feels when encountering something new and unusual.
  4. Shanti (calmness, peace): This Rasa evokes feelings of calmness and peace. It is associated with the sense of inner peace and tranquility that comes from being in harmony with oneself and the world.
  5. Raudra (stress, anger): This Rasa evokes feelings of stress and anger. It is associated with intense emotions such as anger, rage, and fury.
  6. Vira (confidence, power): This Rasa evokes feelings of confidence and power. It is associated with the sense of courage and strength that comes from standing up for oneself and one’s beliefs.
  7. Karuna (sorrow, empathy): This Rasa evokes feelings of sorrow and empathy. It is associated with feelings of compassion and empathy for others, especially in times of suffering.
  8. Bhayanaka (apprehension, anxiety): This Rasa evokes feelings of apprehension and anxiety. It is associated with feelings of fear and uncertainty, especially in the face of danger or threat.
  9. Vibhatsa (self-pity, disgust): This Rasa evokes feelings of self-pity and disgust. It is associated with feelings of revulsion or contempt, especially towards oneself.

These rasas are not mutually exclusive and often overlap and blend with each other; it’s important to note that these are not considered negative emotions but rather a natural and necessary part of the human experience. The goal is not to suppress or avoid these emotions but to understand them and transcend them through spiritual practices.

More information

In some forms of yoga, such as Bhakti yoga, the focus is on cultivating devotion and love for a deity as a way to achieve spiritual transcendence. This kind of yoga is said to evoke the rasa of devotion or love.

In other forms of yoga, such as Raja yoga, the focus is on meditation and concentration as a way to achieve spiritual transcendence. This kind of yoga is said to evoke the rasa of peace or tranquility.

Rasa yoga is a therapeutic yoga style that combines movement, breath, and meditation to help individuals feel more balanced emotionally and mentally. This approach is said to be a way to connect with one’s emotions and to understand and ultimately release emotional blockages.
