What does 'Om Namah Shivaya' mean?

author Bette Green

Om Namah Shivaya is a sacred Hindu mantra, or chant, that is believed to be a powerful invocation of the Hindu god Shiva. The mantra is typically repeated as a form of meditation or as a form of devotion to Shiva. The phrase “Om Namah Shivaya” can be translated to mean “I bow to Shiva” or “I honor the divine within myself.”

The practice of repeating the mantra brings peace, clarity, and connection to the divine. It is often used in yoga and meditation practices to help cultivate a state of inner stillness and focus.

A powerful mantra

Om Namah Shivaya is considered one of the most powerful mantras in Hinduism and is often associated with the god Shiva, who is considered the supreme deity in Shaivism, one of the major branches of Hinduism. The mantra is a powerful tool for spiritual growth and self-realization and is said to bring blessings and grace from Shiva.

The Om sound that starts the mantra is considered the most sacred sound in Hinduism and is believed to be the sound of the universe. It represents the beginning and the end of all things and is said to be the sound that created the universe. The Namah part of the mantra translates to “I honor” or “I bow to,” and the Shivaya part of the mantra is an honorific title for Shiva, which means “the auspicious one” or “the one who is eternally pure and perfect.”

The practice of repeating the Om Namah Shivaya mantra helps purify the mind and body and brings a sense of inner peace and calm. It is said to help the practitioners connect with their own inner divinity and to bring blessings and grace from Shiva.

Om Namah Shivaya is also used in yoga and meditation practices as a way to focus the mind and cultivate a state of inner stillness. It is often recommended to repeat the mantra silently to oneself or out loud during meditation, or as a form of “Japa,” the repetitive recitation of a mantra, which is considered a form of spiritual practice in Hinduism.

In summary, Om Namah Shivaya is a sacred mantra that is believed to be a powerful invocation of the Hindu god Shiva. It is used as a tool for spiritual growth and self-realization and to bring peace and clarity to the mind, body, and spirit. It is a popular practice in Hinduism, yoga, and meditation.
